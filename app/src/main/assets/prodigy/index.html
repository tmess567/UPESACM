  • Prodigy'15

    The Annual Tech Fest
Contact Register

Prodigy is the annual fest organized by UPES-ACM Student Chapter, which strives to create interest and enthusiasm among students for innovation and creativity in the field of technology.
This year, the team took forward this event to an even larger participation and more fun events; while working together to execute its plan to set a milestone.

EVENTS

  • Speaker Program

    1.Distinguished Speaker Program

    Mr. Rudrapatna Shyamsundar, JC Bose National Fellow and a distinguished Professor at IIT Bombay, graced us with his presence in the Distinguished Speaker program. Mr. Shyamsundar, the founder of the Indian Association of Research in Computing Sciences (IARCS) shared his knowledge through a very insightful lecture on the topic "Ensuring Computer and Information Security: Challenges".

    2.Eminent Speaker Program>

    In the Eminent Speaker Program, Mr. Vivek Kulkarni, Chief Architect (Technical Director) of Persistent Labs, Persistent Systems India Limited (Pune), discussed the subject "Theory of Computation" through a very interesting discourse. Mr. Kulkarni, in the past, has shouldered the responsibilities of Senior Product Manager in BMC Software and has worked in organizations such as Symantec Corporation, Tech-Mahindra and Cummins College of Engineering for Women. He is also the inventor of System and Method of Universal Programming Language Conversion. The session gave the students a chance to learn from the industry experts and do away with their doubts.

  • ACM international Coding League

    ACM International Coding League (ACM ICL) was an online one-round coding event, strictly conducted according to the ACM ICPC Rules. The event aimed at testing the coding skills of the participants and indicated their standing among the International programming syndicate. ACM ICL commenced on the 27th March, 2015 at 10:00 PM and was open till 3:00 AM on 28th March, 2015, i.e., 5 hours of hardcore coding for all the coding enthusiasts who participated. The participants were presented with 3 questions in the beginning of the event and a new question was added every hour from 12:00 AM to 2:00 AM. The event was organized on the online platform, HackerEarth. Participation: 315 Winners:
    1st: Swapnil Agarwal (IIT Guwahati)
    2nd: Shantanu Singh Suryavanshi (NIT Kurukshetra)
    3rd: Vibhav Verma (UPES)

    Coding, Quizes, Paper Presentation, Debate
    code rush

    Code rush was a two round coding event, and the participants were in a team of three. In the first round, the coding knowledge of one of the team members was tested through an MCQ test. The second round was a relay race combined with coding challenge. The participants showed high enthusiasm, and found the event to be unique and interesting. Participation: 32 Winners:
    1st: Abhinav Mishra, Sonali Pandey, Akash Ranjan
    2nd: Aadarsh, Raghav Garg, Vivek
    3rd: Raghav Dua, Siddhanth Kapoor, Parth Gupta

    Generation Web

    Web development skills of the participants were tested in the event. With a list of some basic requirements, the participants were given 24 hours to accomplish the task. The participants only had to design the basic html layout of the website using CSS, JavaScript, .NET, HTML, XML and jquery. The authenticity of the same was tested by the judges by a surprise question session to the participants. Generation Web was all about honouring the creative, technically proficient & future business-savvy Web designers who can't help but believe, "Talk is cheap, show me the code!" Participation: 16 Winners:
    1st: Vipul Sharma
    2nd: Shubham Sharma
