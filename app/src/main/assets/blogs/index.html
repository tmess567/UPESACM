  • Advantage of Cloud Computing

    During the past few years, cloud computing has become a key IT buzzword. Although the definition of cloud computing is still "cloudy", the trade press and bloggers label many vendors as cloud computing vendors, and report on their services and issues. Cloud computing is in its infancy in terms of market adoption. However, it is a key IT megatrend that will take root. The article would give you a glimpse of advantages of cloud computing which would implies why the cloud computing have expanding their roots. Here are some the advantages that I have explored. Firstly very common The users can increase or decrease the level of use of the computing resources and services flexibly and easily. Secondly The 3rd party provider owns and manages all the computing resources (servers, software, storage, and networking) and electricity needed for the services. The users only need to "plug into" the cloud. The users do not need to make a large upfront investment on computing resources; the space needed to house them; electricity needed to run the computing resources; and the cost of maintaining staff for administering the system, network, and database. Moreover The users pay most likely much less for the services, because they pay only for the computing resources and services they use, and the subscription-based or pay-per-use charges are likely much lower than the cost of maintaining on-premises computing resources. If the users are to maintain on-premises computing resources, they also need to make the worst-case plan to account for the occasional or seasonal peak needs. And also it provide the facility to acess the cloud anytime from anywhere.

  • Cloud Computing - A Brief Introduction

    In 1961, John McCathy, for the first time publicly suggested that the computing power and the information technology can be delivered as a utility to business, much like the electricity and later in 2000s, it became a reality when cloud computing came into the business. Cloud computing is not a new technology but an old one which in very simple words is a way for delivering IT as a Service to the business. It is an IT service framework which is getting more popularity due to the various benefits it provide over the tradition IT infrastructure. Cloud computing brings savings to the business due to various factors such as virtualization, consolidation, scalability, flexibility, ease of manageability, automation, tenacity, pay as per use etc.

    Virtualization technology acted as the key enabler for cloud computing platform by introducing infrastructure flexibility i.e. the costumers need not to own their personal hardware to access the cloud services, easy change management i.e. it is easy to change the service provider and to get things managed on the cloud platform, easy administration at the client side as well as the provider side as everything is automated and low cost solution factor as one only has to pay for the service and resources they use (even few services are free of cost such as the google drop box) etc.

    Cloud can be classified on the basis of two criteria: (1) the manner in which it is deployed i.e. the cloud deployment models and (2) the layer on which it operates i.e. the cloud delivery models. There are 3 cloud deployment models namely Private cloud, Public cloud and Hybrid cloud. Also there are 3 delivery models Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Software as a Service (SaaS).

    Switching from the traditional IT infrastructure to the cloud computing platform is also easy. The transformation roadmap comprise of three easy steps: (1) Consolidation and Virtualization which involves moving from the pure physical infrastructure to a virtual infrastructure i.e. all the basic asset management, monitoring and optimization is done in a virtualized environment using virtualization tools (2) Automation and Optimization which includes recording the configuration of various components in set up infrastructure recorded in a centralized database and enabling faster problem resolution (3) Integration of Service Management which involves setting up a Service Catalog for the services available in the cloud which may be used on-demand and on a self-service basis.

  • The World of 4G

    In today’s world 4g is developed and came into market. But what is 4g? And how it is different from 3g and 2g? In this article we will explore the technologies are different from each other.

    4G is basically stood for fourth generation mobile technology and the same is applied for 3G and 2G. As 4G is succeeding 3G therefore it provides some more feature over 3G for example it provides mobile broadband Internet access, for example to laptops with wireless modems to smartphones, and to other mobile devices. Potential and current applications include amended mobile web access, IP telephony, gaming services, high definition mobile TV, video conferencing and cloud computing. And not only by these services a network is considered as 4G but there are some standards which have been set by International Telecommunications Union-Radio communications sector . According to which the network should provide the above services at 100 megabits per second for higher mobility communication. And 1gegabits per second for low mobility communication. The second releases by mobile WI-max and LTE are meeting these requirements and hence these two operators introduce the world with 4g technology. These are the technological basis of a 4G network spectrum. But to understand the difference between these generations we have to get back into 1980’s.

    As the network systems were set up in 1980 and the spectrum was distributed for various networking communities this generation is termed as the 1st generation and from then as research is improving the speed and services of network are also improving and from then approximately after each 10 years we get a new generation. This is the story behind the generation of networking.

    But soon we got a next generation that is 5G in which we get a speed of 1gegabits per second in higher mobility communication the research for these types of networking has already being carried out at Spain and United States of America.
